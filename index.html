The Artist's Life

Van Gogh's Journey

1885

Dutch Roots

Van Gogh began his artistic career in the Netherlands, focusing on rural themes and earthy tones. His first major work, The Potato Eaters, captured the harsh, honest life of peasants and marked his early interest in social realism and the dignity of common people.

1887

Parisian Influence

In Paris, Van Gogh encountered Impressionism and Japanese prints, which revolutionized his use of color and light. He painted vibrant portraits like Portrait of Père Tanguy, reflecting this dramatic artistic shift from dark, somber tones to the brilliant palette that would define his later work.

1888

The Arles Period

Van Gogh moved to Arles seeking better light and warmer weather. Here he created some of his most famous works, including the Sunflowers series and The Bedroom.

1889

Saint-Rémy Asylum

After his mental breakdown and the famous ear incident, Van Gogh voluntarily entered the Saint-Rémy asylum. Despite his struggles, this period produced masterpieces like The Starry Night and Irises, showcasing his unique ability to transform personal turmoil into artistic brilliance.
